Edge Of The Web

HTML | CSS | JavaScript | Illustration | UX Design | Branding

This is an exploration of the challenges, the experiences and the outcomes I faced while working on this project throughout my internship at Humaan.

Project Overview

1.

Create a refreshed, approachable and modern identity and brand for Edge of the Web.

2.

Propose an accessible website design that is informative, interesting and in-line with the branding.

3.

Build a fully responsive, robust website with all of the intended functionality.

4.

Create a user experience that is engaging, without being distracting.

Process and Challenges

This brief was intended as a learning exercise and as a way of gaining experience working in a collaborative environment, such as a design studio. I faced many challenges throughout the end to end process.

Overcoming these challenges was rewarding and enlightening.

Outcomes and Learning

Due to the scope of this project, I encountered many hurdles. Some of which were easily overcome and others had the devs scratching their heads too.

I believe that the measure of success for this project is a self-reflective one. I will attempt to answer it as conscisely as I can in the follow 3 questions.

Was the design process a challenge?

Yes, yes it was. I felt that I was under a lot of pressure to propose a highly considered approach. Being my first step into an agency environment and being surrounded by talented designers and developers was both the biggest help, but also the source of a lot of the pressure.

Receiving feedback throughout the design process was the key to the creation of the final designs. I have gained a massive respect for the power of feedback and collaboration and will endevour to use it whenever I can in the future.

Presenting to the team at Humaan was hugely rewarding as not only did I receive expert feedback and advice, but I gained experience explaining and justifying my design choices. I gained a lot of confidence in my ability to design well, and to listen to critique.

Did I learn new practices, techniques or skills while prototyping and building?

Of course I did. While designing I tried not to tie myself down within what I knew to be my limitations of coding ability. Instead I pushed myself to learn what I needed to to replicate my designs in code as close as possible.

Refining my usage of code like flex-box and GSAP were only one part of my successes. I also taught myself to code an accordion menu from scratch, and built a infinite scroll loop function with the help of a few of the devs (although this one had everyone scratching their heads for a while).

Was it fun?

It was great! I genuinely enjoyed the challenge throughout the entire process. I had a lot of fun learning about new technologies and code snippets that could help and completing the final build was very rewarding.
